Step 3: model, training core, and synthetic sanity check

model.py: bidirectional LSTM (hidden 128, 3 layers, dropout 0.4) + temporal
attention + dual classification/regression head.
train.py: reusable set_seed / fit (dual loss, early stopping) / predict.
interpret.py: faithful gradient saliency (temporal + channel) plus an attention
readout flagged as unfaithful.
synthetic_check.py: separable curves with signal confined to early bins.

Key finding from the sanity check: the model classifies the synthetic data
perfectly (acc/AUC = 1.0) but its ATTENTION is flat and does NOT localize to the
signal bins, across every encoder depth/direction tested. Input-gradient
SALIENCY does localize (77% of mass on the signal bins, peak at bin 0, recovering
the injected decay). So saliency, not attention, is the faithful temporal
readout. This is exactly the bug the sanity check exists to catch; we keep the
attention layer but base scientific claims on saliency.
